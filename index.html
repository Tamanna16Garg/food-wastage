Introduction

Food waste is an escalating issue at VIT Bhopal University, mirroring a global challenge. On campus, it is estimated that nearly 25% of the food prepared for students is either wasted or lost each month. This amounts to approximately 1,060 kg of food waste, a quantity that could easily feed 4,000 people. Despite this, many students remain unaware of the severity of the issue.

Globally, around one-third of food produced for human consumption is wasted, and the situation at VIT Bhopal reflects this troubling trend. The impact of such waste is not just a matter of lost resources but also highlights a lack of awareness and education around food conservation. Addressing this issue is crucial for both reducing waste and fostering a more sustainable campus environment.

Why This Project Matters

Food waste is a significant issue that has far-reaching consequences for the environment, economy, and society. By tracking and reducing food waste at VIT Bhopal University, we aim to:

  • Reduce greenhouse gas emissions associated with food production and waste.
  • Conserve water and energy resources used in food production.
  • Minimize financial losses due to overproduction and spoilage.
  • Promote sustainable practices within the university community.
  • Raise awareness about the importance of reducing food waste at the individual level.

Preventive Measures

To reduce food waste, we recommend the following preventive measures:

  • Implement portion control to reduce overproduction.
  • Improve inventory management to minimize spoilage.
  • Educate staff on best practices for food preparation and storage.
  • Introduce waste tracking systems to monitor and address inefficiencies.
  • Encourage students to take only what they can consume.
  • Donate surplus food to local charities to prevent waste.
